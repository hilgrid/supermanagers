# 30 Days of AI

A daily exercise program to build AI fluency. Each exercise takes 10-15 minutes. Work through them in order - each week builds on the last.

---

## Week 1: Foundations of LLM Interaction

### Day 1: Get Better Outputs by Providing Context
Learn how providing detailed context can significantly improve the relevance and specificity of AI answers, helping you get better recommendations and insights.

1. Begin by prompting, "Help me plan an upcoming vacation. What information can I give you that would be the most helpful?" The AI will respond with a variety of follow-up questions. Answer as many as you like and see what sorts of answers you get.
2. Prompt, "Let me tell you about some of the best vacations I've ever had." Add specific context, like the names of hotels or restaurants you've enjoyed.
3. Prompt, "Your suggestions are too _____, make them more ____." Use a variety of adjectives: too generic, expensive, touristy, predictable, etc. Notice how this changes the output.
4. Prompt, "Let's go with [destination]. Ask me three questions that will allow you to build the perfect itinerary for me." The AI will ask you three questions and then suggest an itinerary for you.
5. Prompt, "Make this 100x more specific." Notice how this changes the output.

### Day 2: Get Ideas for Use Cases Relevant to Your Role
Brainstorm use cases for AI that are specific to your role.

1. Prompt, "I'm a [job] at [company] focusing on [domain] and I am trying to identify use cases for using AI. Can you suggest some non-obvious ideas? Be extremely specific."
2. If you have any doubts about the ideas it provides, explain those doubts. For example, if it generates ideas about using AI to write copy, you could say, "I feel like AI writes generic and uncanny sounding copy," "focus on something other than writing" or simply "I don't like these ideas."
3. Prompt, "Give me an extremely specific step by step guide to implementing the best use case here, including any prompts I should use."
4. Prompt, "What changes would you make if I told you I have an extremely high bar for quality and will accept nothing but the best? Explain your reasoning."

### Day 3: Summarize and Simplify Complex Documents
Quickly understand dense or complex documents by having AI summarize key sections and clarify technical or difficult parts.

1. Upload a document (e.g., PDF or Google Doc) and type, "Please summarize the key points, in a way that will be helpful for me, a [title] at [company]."
2. Prompt, "Make this even more helpful and actionable for me."
3. Prompt, "What are the three most important and non obvious insights I should take away from this, and what action should I take as a result?"
4. Prompt, "Make those even more specific and personalized for me, a [job title] who focuses on [domain]."
5. Review the summary and continue asking any other questions you have to shape and clarify the output, for example, "Make those recommendations more creative," or "highlight less obvious insights."

### Day 4: Break Down a Project into Steps
Figure out a structured approach to solving a problem or making a plan.

1. Share your project goal and deadline with the AI, asking, "Can you suggest different ways to break down this project into manageable parts?"
2. Once you have some options, ask, "Which of these breakdowns would be most efficient given a [timeline/limited team]?"
3. Review the feedback, select a breakdown approach, and then ask the AI to recommend milestones or checkpoints to track progress.
4. Use this structured approach to manage the project and adjust as you go.

### Day 5: Create a Pros/Cons List for a Decision
Describe an upcoming decision and ask the AI to help you create a structured pros and cons list, with weighted factors for each point.

1. Describe the decision you're facing and any key factors. Ask, "Can you suggest some frameworks to evaluate this decision?"
2. Choose a framework you like and then say, "Help me list the pros and cons of each option, factoring in [specific criteria like time, resources, potential impact]."
3. Review the list, and if needed, ask for weighted factors to better understand the trade-offs.
4. Use the refined list to guide your final decision-making process.

### Day 6: Identify AI Use Cases Based on Your Calendar
Identify relevant AI tools and strategies for your upcoming meetings by sharing your calendar with the AI, ensuring you have tailored support for each task.

1. Take a screenshot of your calendar for the week. Paste it in and prompt, "I have a busy schedule this week, and I'd like to use AI to help me prepare effectively for each of my meetings. I am attaching a screenshot of my calendar. Can you suggest specific AI strategies or approaches that I can use to maximize the value of each meeting? For example, preparing talking points, generating questions, brainstorming creative ideas, or summarizing key takeaways. I'd like actionable and non-obvious suggestions for each meeting to make sure I contribute effectively and stay organized."
2. Choose one idea you'd like to try. Prompt, "I'd like to try [idea]. Recommend a step-by-step approach to implementing this. Write out any AI prompts I should use and clarify any additional context I would need to provide the AI to get a good output."
3. Prompt, "I don't like any of these ideas" and notice how the AI responds.
4. Choose one idea and commit to trying it out this week.

### Day 7: Develop a Checklist for Reviewing Work
Describe a type of work you frequently review (like code, writing, or designs) and ask the AI to help you create a checklist for quality and consistency.

1. Describe the type of work you typically review (e.g., reports, code). Ask, "Can you suggest an initial checklist for quality and consistency in this area?"
2. Review the checklist and say, "What additional criteria should I add to capture common issues I see, like [specific issues]?"
3. Ask for examples of how to evaluate each checklist item, ensuring clarity.
4. Save the refined checklist and update it over time as you identify new review needs.

## Week 2: Improving Your Communication with AI

### Day 8: Increase the Clarity of Your Written Communication
Make your writing easier to understand. Use AI to identify and revise any ambiguous language or confusing sentence structures.

1. Copy and paste a message you are preparing to send over email or Slack.
2. Prompt, "I am [a product manager] sending this message to [a marketing manager]. Can you edit it for clarity, and explain any changes you made?"
3. Review the response. If it sounds too formal or AI-generated, provide that feedback: "This sounds like a machine wrote it. Can you make it sound more like me?"
4. Once you have a version you like, ask "On a scale from 1-5, how clear is this message?"
5. Finalize the message and send it with confidence.

### Day 9: Structure a Presentation
Get help identifying the best structure for an upcoming presentation.

1. Describe the main points and purpose of your presentation. Ask, "Can you suggest multiple ways to structure this presentation to best engage my audience?"
2. Pick a structure you like, and ask, "What types of visuals or examples would help strengthen each section?"
3. Use the suggestions to draft your presentation, then ask for feedback on specific sections you want to improve.
4. Refine your presentation based on the AI's ideas for a more engaging delivery.

### Day 10: Generate a First Draft from an Outline
Speed up the drafting process by expanding a rough outline into a more complete draft. AI can help with organization, filling gaps, and adding depth to your ideas.

1. Without using AI, write a basic outline with bullet points for each section of your document.
2. Share the outline and say, "I'm working on [topic] to achieve [goal]. Here's my draft outline. What improvements can you suggest, like structure changes or filling gaps?"
3. Review the additions, and integrate relevant suggestions.
4. If the result feels too general, prompt it with, "Can you make the introduction more engaging?" or "Add concrete examples to each section."
5. Refine the draft further by adding your own insights or expertise, making it better aligned with your style and goals.

### Day 11: Generate Key Points for an Executive Summary
After drafting a document, distill the key points into a clear and concise executive summary.

1. Provide a draft or outline of your report. Ask, "Can you identify the most critical points for an executive summary?"
2. Once you have key points, say, "Help me refine these points into concise sentences that are impactful and clear."
3. Review the summary and ask, "From the point of view of [audience], what questions or objections might you have?" Then ask, "How can I update my summary to address these?"
4. Finalize the summary for a polished, executive-ready version of your report.

### Day 12: Iterate on Drafts to Improve Quality
Learn how iterative, back-and-forth feedback can improve your writing quality.

1. Write a rough draft of your document or message and paste it in.
2. Ask, "Can you review this draft for tone, structure, and clarity, and suggest specific improvements to make it more engaging and clear?"
3. Review the feedback, focusing on actionable suggestions (e.g., reordering sections, simplifying language, or enhancing key points).
4. Revise your draft based on the feedback and paste the updated version back in. Ask, "Here's the revised version. Can you now check for flow and ensure it's concise and impactful?"
5. Continue refining through several iterations, with each round focusing on different improvements, like strengthening the introduction or adding concrete examples, until you're satisfied with the final draft.
6. Zoom out with a more vague and general question like, "What am I missing?" See if you get any interesting feedback.

### Day 13: Get Feedback on Visual Design
Use AI to evaluate your slides, designs, reports, or other visual content, helping you refine design elements, improve layout, and enhance clarity.

1. Take a screenshot of your visual content or download it as a PDF and upload it. This could be a slide deck, a single slide, a Figma design, a diagram, etc.
2. Ask, "What feedback can you give me on the layout, clarity, and audience engagement of this visual?"
3. Review the feedback, noting any specific improvement suggestions.
4. If the feedback is vague, prompt with, "Be 100x more specific. Which elements, like color, font size, or spacing, could improve readability?"
5. If the feedback isn't aligned with your needs, clarify the context by explaining your goal, audience, guidelines, or any past discussions on this topic.
6. Apply the revised feedback to make your visual clearer and more effective for your audience.

### Day 14: Provide Colleagues with the Best Answer to Their Questions
Get help breaking down complex questions when you're not sure how to proceed.

1. Prompt, "I am a product manager who just received this question from an engineer and I want to ensure I come up with the best possible answer in the next 3 minutes. Provide a step by step guide for how I should determine the answer: 'What's the acceptable latency threshold for [feature], and what's our plan if latency increases during peak usage times?'"
2. Prompt, "Can you suggest an initial target based on similar features or benchmarks? Show your work so I can validate it."
3. Prompt, "Give me a better answer, and explain why it's better."
4. Ask, "Imagine you are [the person who sent you the question]. What pushback might you have to this response?"

### Bonus: Help Craft "No" Responses That Maintain Relationships
When you need to decline a request, share the context and ask, "Can you help me say no in a way that keeps the relationship positive?"

1. Start by sharing the request and providing context, e.g., who the request is coming from, whether you feel like you have the authority to say no, why it doesn't fit your current priorities. Ask, "What are some alternative ways I could support this person's needs without taking on the full ask?" and "What are some questions I can ask this person to better understand how urgent/important this ask is?"
2. Explore the suggestions. Then ask, "Can you help me write a response that both suggests these alternatives and keeps the relationship positive?"
3. After receiving the response, ask how the other person might respond to your message, and what you can do to improve the interaction.
4. Finalize the response and keep note of any useful phrasing for future "no" responses.

### Bonus: Complete Documents in Your Style
Draft a framework and initial content, then use AI to complete the remaining sections in a style that matches your own, ensuring consistency and polish.

1. Set up your document by writing an outline, creating sections and bullet points to guide the structure.
2. Draft the first 20% by writing a solid introduction and some initial content to set the tone, style, and voice.
3. Paste your outline and initial draft in, and say, "Please complete the remaining sections, using my style and structure as a basis."
4. Review the AI's additions to ensure they match your tone. If adjustments are needed, ask, "Can you make this more [concise, formal, detailed, etc.] to better align with my writing?"

### Bonus: Summarize Meeting Takeaways to Keep Everyone Informed
Create a concise, actionable summary of meeting takeaways and decisions, then share broadly to increase transparency.

1. Copy detailed meeting notes (with speaker attributions if possible) and paste them in.
2. Say, "Distill these notes into a clear, structured summary, including key decisions, rationale, highlights, and specific action items with ownership and deadlines."
3. Review and edit the summary to ensure accuracy and alignment with meeting outcomes.
4. Share the summary with the group, requesting a "thumbs up" for alignment on takeaways and next steps.
5. Share the summary with anyone who was not in the meeting who could benefit from the context.

## Week 3: Improving Creativity and Strategy

### Day 15: Prepare Talking Points for Meetings
Improve your contributions in meetings by generating relevant insights, potential questions, or key points that align with the meeting's goals.

1. Provide the meeting agenda and your role, saying, "I have a meeting about [topic]. Here's the agenda and my role: [details]."
2. Ask, "What are some specific points or questions I could bring up to add value to this discussion?"
3. Review the suggestions and highlight any that resonate with you or fit your expertise.
4. If suggestions feel off-track, add context by explaining your point of view, notes from prior meetings, other attendees, or specific concerns you have about the meeting.
5. Ask for help preparing a few selected talking points or questions to contribute meaningfully to the meeting.

### Day 16: Prioritize Tasks Based on Impact
Use AI to prioritize tasks or ideas effectively by applying a scoring framework, ensuring you focus on the highest-impact efforts.

1. List your tasks or ideas and paste them in.
2. Ask, "Can you prioritize these tasks based on urgency and impact, focusing on the most high-impact items first?"
3. If needed, ask, "What additional information would you need from me to prioritize more confidently?" and provide the requested context.
4. Say, "Can you suggest prioritization criteria and score each item based on those criteria?"

### Day 17: 10x Ideas After a Brainstorm
Get more out of brainstorming sessions by generating additional ideas or exploring variations, providing new angles to consider.

1. Share a list of core ideas from your brainstorming session, saying, "Here are our main ideas: [list ideas]."
2. Ask, "Can you generate 30 additional ideas or suggest variations worth exploring? Ensure these ideas are [excellent/unique/thoughtful/creative]." Try different words and see what happens. You can even give the AI a persona, like, "You are a Creative Director at a prestigious Ad Agency. Come up with your 50 most creative ideas" and see how this impacts the quality of the ideas you get.
3. Ask, "Can you set scoring criteria for the quality of these ideas, score each one, and prioritize the top ten?"
4. If ideas are repetitive, prompt with, "Make these ideas 100x more unique," or "Focus on adding innovative twists to each one."

### Day 18: Kickstart Research on New Topics
Begin researching a new topic by generating essential questions, guiding you to explore foundational concepts and issues deeply.

1. Enter a broad topic you want to explore (e.g., "healthcare economics" or "behavioral change"). Ask, "What are the essential questions I should research to gain a deep understanding of this topic?"
2. Use the list of questions as a guide, selecting 2-3 to start with and diving into those first. Ask the AI to answer those questions and also recommend the best and most credible resources to learn more.
3. As you research, return for help refining answers or to explore follow-up questions.

### Day 19: Build a Personalized Learning Path
Design a structured learning path for a new topic by outlining key concepts, modules, and resources, tailored to your development goals.

1. Prompt, "What are the key concepts I should understand to build a basic foundation in [domain]?" If the response seems irrelevant to your role, follow up with, "Can you make this more specific to my role as [job] at [company]?" Provide any additional context that would be helpful, like how you will use this information in your role.
2. Prompt, "Can you create a step-by-step learning path covering these concepts, with modules and milestones for each stage?"
3. Prompt, "Break down the first module into smaller, manageable lessons or exercises. Include specific resources I can use and explain how to use them. Define any words I am unlikely to understand as a beginner."

### Day 20: Create a Targeted Professional Growth Plan for Yourself
Develop a targeted growth plan to build specific skills. Get actionable deliverables and feedback questions to track your progress effectively.

1. Prompt, "I am a [product manager] focused on [domain]. I would like to improve my [strategic thinking] over the next three months. What specific skills or behaviors help me do this while making a tangible impact in my role?"
2. Prompt, "Suggest 3 specific deliverables I could work on over the next month to practice these skills in a way that adds value to [my company]." If the output is too generic, say "Make this 100x more specific."
3. Choose one deliverable and ask, "Is there a more lightweight way to approach [idea] so it doesn't become a big project that distracts me from short-term execution?"
4. Prompt, "Can you suggest questions I could ask my manager to get feedback on my progress along the way?"

### Day 21: Brainstorm Multiple Approaches to Solve a Problem
Outline a problem you're facing and ask, "Can you suggest several distinct approaches to tackle this?" to encourage creative thinking.

1. Outline the problem and any constraints. Ask, "What are a few different approaches I could take to solve this problem?"
2. Review the suggestions, then ask, "What are the potential benefits and downsides of each approach?"
3. If you find an approach interesting but incomplete, ask, "Can you expand on this approach with specific steps or examples?"
4. Choose an approach to implement and revisit it if adjustments are needed.

## Week 4: Advanced Prompting and Using Voice

### Day 22: Ask an LLM to Write Its Own Prompt
Learn to lean on the AI to craft specialized prompts. This exercise helps you become more precise and effective in guiding AI responses.

1. Prompt, "I want to generate new ideas for [something you are working on]. Can you help me write a prompt that will effectively achieve this goal?"
2. Follow up with, "Ask me three questions that would give you helpful information to improve the prompt."
3. Answer each question, or simply say, "What would you recommend as answers to each question if I were unsure?"
4. Prompt, "Now that we have these details, can you update the prompt and explain what you changed so I can understand how to write better prompts?"
5. Read the updated prompt and notice the structure and content of it.

### Day 23: Refine Ideas with Voice-to-Text Exploration
Use voice mode to talk through early-stage ideas, turning rough thoughts into structured concepts. This approach helps improve clarity and expression for brainstorming.

1. Open voice mode on your AI's mobile app and say, "I have an idea I'd like to refine with your help."
2. Describe your idea out loud, as if explaining it to a colleague or mentor.
3. Ask for clarifying questions that could help refine or sharpen the concept.
4. If responses are too verbose, remind it to stay concise and focused, saying, "Keep your questions brief and specific."
5. After 5 minutes of back-and-forth, ask it to outline a one-pager to present the idea to your manager, specifying their role and what would appeal to them.

### Day 24: Get Hyper-Personalized Recommendations Based on Your Taste
Use examples to articulate what "good" looks like in your eyes.

1. Gather a set of examples that reflect your preferences (e.g., outfits, books, designs) and upload or describe them.
2. Ask, "Based on these examples, can you analyze my taste and describe it in very specific terms?"
3. Once you get a description of your taste, say, "Using these specific characteristics, write a prompt that will help me get personalized recommendations that match my style."
4. Use the generated prompt to ask for personalized recommendations that align with your unique taste.
5. Review the recommendations and refine the prompt if needed, asking to make adjustments like "focus more on [specific elements you like]."

### Day 25: Reflect on Your Core Values
Engage in a guided voice conversation to uncover and clarify your core values through easy-to-answer, reflective questions.

1. Start by enabling voice mode and saying, "I want to clarify my core values. Can you ask me specific, easy-to-answer questions, one at a time, to keep this conversational?"
2. Begin the conversation, responding to one question at a time. Skip any questions you don't want to answer or don't know the answer to.
3. After several rounds, ask it to summarize the recurring themes and values it identified, using specific words and phrases from your responses to ensure it captures your language and perspective.
4. Provide feedback on whether the summary feels true to you or not, and why.

### Day 26: Talk Through Problems for Clarity
Use voice mode to work through a problem where you feel stuck. The AI will guide you with reflective questions, helping you gain clarity and possible next steps.

1. Think of any challenge you are facing in your professional or personal life. If you're not sure, make one up.
2. Open voice mode on the mobile app. Say, "I'm feeling stuck on a problem, and I'd like to talk it through with you. Can you act as my coach and help me work through the problem?"
3. Say, "I am talking to you on voice mode, so let's keep this conversational."
4. Describe the problem and how you are feeling about it.
5. As you explain, the AI will listen and offer prompts to help you dig deeper. If the conversation is not going in a helpful direction, say that.
6. After a couple minutes, ask it to summarize any key insights or new perspectives that emerged during the discussion.
7. Say, "Can you suggest a few next steps based on our conversation?" to help you move forward with a clearer plan.

### Day 27: Prepare for Difficult Conversations
Prepare for challenging conversations by simulating potential scenarios, helping you approach interpersonal dynamics with empathy and grace.

1. Describe the situation. For example, you might say, "I need to talk to my colleague about missed deadlines without making them feel defensive."
2. Ask for suggestions on how to bring up the topic with your colleague.
3. Choose one suggestion to "play out" and ask, "Help me anticipate their possible reactions and suggest graceful responses for each."
4. Continue playing out the scenario, asking for options and alternate approaches as needed.
5. Request specific phrases by asking, "What wording can I use to stay calm and respectful?"

### Day 28: Reverse-Engineer Prompts to Make LLMs That Think Like You
Improve your prompting skills by reverse-engineering the transformation from a rough draft to a final draft. This exercise helps you craft prompts for specific outcomes.

1. Start with two drafts: choose a piece of your writing with both a rough first draft and a refined final version (e.g., an initial idea and a polished proposal). Upload both.
2. Ask it to describe the major differences between the first and final draft.
3. Say, "Write a prompt that would take the first draft as an input and transform it into something like the final draft." This will help the AI understand how to replicate the changes.
4. Test the prompt: use the prompt on a new rough draft to see if it achieves a similar transformation. Refine the prompt if needed by asking for ways to make it more effective.
